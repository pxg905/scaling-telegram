How C.D.B.I. Ensures Accuracy

C.D.B.I. (Canadian Digital Ballot Initiative) is a revolutionary digital voting system designed for federal elections. It operates exactly like a traditional election in terms of process, but replaces outdated paper methods with a secure, transparent, and verifiable digital architecture.

The Challenge: Eliminating Error

Traditional voting systems are susceptible to human error—from miscounts and uncounted ballots to the lack of immediate, secure verification that a vote was tallied correctly. These inaccuracies can crucially affect election outcomes, especially when single votes decide a seat in the House of Commons.

C.D.B.I solves this by making the entire ballot process digital and linking every vote to an immutable, decentralized blockchain ledger.

Architecture: Digital Ballots and Identity

Encrypted Digital Identity

Every registered SIN (Social Insurance Number) holder generates a single, encrypted digital voting token. This token is securely linked to the voter's identity via the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) credentials, ensuring one vote per citizen.

Offline Operation & CRA Data

Crucially, the system is designed not to be dependent on the public internet during the vote. All necessary voter registration data from the CRA is securely uploaded to the operating system of the ATM Voting Machine (AVM) beforehand, ensuring uninterrupted, protected access during voting hours.

The Voting Procedure (ATM Kiosk)

  1. 1.

    Authentication

    At the ATM Voting Machine (AVM), you are prompted to enter your SIN number and complete a 2-step verification using your standard CRA credentials for high security.

  2. 2.

    Candidate Selection

    Once verified, the interface displays the Candidate Voting Screen. This touch-screen interface clearly shows the Party Name and the Leader's Name. The system is also headphone accessible for all voters.

  3. 3.

    Confirmation

    The next screen is the Confirmation Interface. It displays your selected vote and requires explicit acceptance. Crucially, once accepted, no further changes to that ballot are possible.

  4. 4.

    Verification & Exit

    The final Overview Confirmation page confirms your vote has been received and verified on the blockchain. You are given the option to print a physical receipt of your verified transaction ID before hitting 'Exit'. The AVM resets to the welcome screen for the next voter.

Blockchain: Security, Transparency, and Real-Time Results

  • Immutability: The entire voting system is run on a decentralized blockchain ledger, making every vote tamper-proof and guaranteeing safety and security.
  • Real-Time Transparency: Results are displayed on a national dashboard in real-time as votes are cast and validated on the blockchain, eliminating long wait times and ensuring maximum public transparency.
